What are you struggling with?

Prototype passes bench tests but fails in production:

  • intermittent sensor drift on assembly,
  • inconsistent calibrations,
  • no repeatable test fixture,
  • first 100 units failing environmental/EMC checks.

Scaling startup and ramping from pilot to 1k+/month:

  • yield drops when set to higher throughput,
  • operator-dependent assembly steps,
  • missing SOPs and test jigs,
  • sudden supplier part variances breaking line flow.

Upgrade & retrofit. Legacy line requires a new product variant:

  • 90-minute changeovers,
  • robotic cell misalignment causing scrap,
  • obsolete spare parts,
  • inability to integrate automated inspection without major CAPEX.

Project Showcase

  • Bicycle Lock Holder

    Scale: Hobby

    In this project I overcome bicycle frame limitations and adjusted to user riding style. Inspiration of the design came from open-source designs on Thingiverse.com and Printables.com.

  • Measuring Fixture

    Scale: Medium

    Quality control tool for periodic dimensional and tolerance check of special product characteristic in serial production. Fixture adapted for regular production personnel with minimal training needed.

  • Automatic Assembly Line

    Scale: Big

    Absolute beast producing millions of electromechanical switches per year. Automated parts feeding, inbuilt calibration and final quality control. Designed from zero. My impact was in pneumatic system design, mechanics optimisation, algorithm and HMI architecture. Full-scale launch tests and hand over to serial production support included.

    Scale: Medium

    Typical equipment for automated production lines. Two assembly operations combined on one carriage, inbuilt process quality control, passive and active safety features.

    Scale: Hobby

    Prototype of custom trays to carry different kind of stationery. The problem to resolve was limited storage space. Inspired by open-source designs on Thingiverse.com and Printables.com
